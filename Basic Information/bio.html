Biography

Michael Ogbeide is a passionate advocate and enthusiast for financial inclusion and women-focused banking. He is committed to expanding access to financial services in underserved and underbanked communities, with a special focus on promoting women's participation in the financial sector. Through his work, Michael has provided platforms to support women-led SMEs and drive inclusive finance initiatives.

His career began in business, but he later transitioned into the financial technology space, driven by a deep-rooted desire to ensure no one is left behind in accessing financial services. Today, he plays a pivotal role in advancing technologies used in agency banking and digital finance delivery.

Holds a Bachelor's degree in Religious Studies from the University of Benin, Benin City, and is a member of the Nigerian Institute of Management. His academic and early career experiences in societal and environmental advocacy laid the foundation for his entry into the financial sector, where he has continued to champion causes that improve lives.

Recognizing that transforming the financial sector is no easy task, Michael has continually upskilled himself at ALT School Africa, acquiring the technical knowledge and tools necessary to design and implement innovative financial solutions. His work focuses on improving service delivery, developing reliable systems, and driving technological advancements to deepen financial inclusion.

His current goals include building solutions that eliminate transaction failures, enable real-time settlements (including for foreign card transactions on local POS systems), and significantly reduce the long queues at ATMs and banking halls.
